Maintenance Tips (Better Cooling & Hygiene)

  • Pre-season pad replacement and deep tank cleaning for maximum cooling
  • Use RO/soft water; for hard water, apply anti-scale treatment
  • Flush the tank weekly during peak season to prevent algae and odor build-up
  • Ensure proper cross-ventilation (fresh air intake)

FAQs

How often should cooling pads be replaced?

For heavy usage, replacing pads before summer (March–April) is ideal. Honeycomb pads typically last 1–2 seasons, while wood wool pads last 1 season.

Why does cooling reduce with hard water?

Mineral deposits clog the pads, causing uneven water distribution. Anti-scale cleaning and pad replacement restore performance.

What causes excessive noise in coolers?

It may be due to fan imbalance, worn motor bearings, belt tension issues (in ducted coolers), or loose body panels. Proper inspection and alignment fix these issues.

How are service charges calculated?

Basic inspection/diagnosis has a nominal fee. Consumables (pads, pumps, capacitors, wheels, etc.) and specific repairs are charged separately after estimate approval.
